Projects

Sentiment Analysis

This project performs sentiment analysis on 40,000 tweets to uncover trends in public opinion and social media engagement. Tweets were classified as Positive, Negative, or Neutral, revealing the prevailing emotional tone of the dataset. Exploratory analysis included sentiment distribution, top contributing authors, and text insights such as tweet length, frequent words, and bigrams. Findings show that most tweets are short and clustered around trending topics, with a few highly active authors influencing overall sentiment. Keyword and bigram analysis across sentiments highlights emotional triggers, supporting targeted social media monitoring and reporting.

Customer Lifetime Value

This project analyzes the drivers of Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) using a dataset of 1,000 customers, focusing on purchase history, tenure, and total spending. Exploratory analysis revealed a highly skewed CLV distribution, where a small segment of high-value customers contributes disproportionately to overall revenue. Correlation analysis showed that total spending is the strongest predictor of CLV, followed by purchase frequency and tenure. Segmentation into low, medium, and high CLV tiers provides actionable insights for retention, upselling, and reactivation strategies, with predictive modeling further enabling data-driven targeting of high-value customers.

Customer Churn

This project analyzes and predicts customer churn for a retail bank using a dataset of 10,000 customers with demographic, account, and engagement features. Through exploratory data analysis, key churn patterns were identified, including higher exit rates among older customers, lower satisfaction scores, and specific geographic segments. Predictive modeling with scikit-learn was implemented to classify customers at risk of leaving, enabling data-driven retention strategies. Insights from this analysis can guide targeted interventions such as loyalty programs, personalized offers, and proactive engagement to reduce churn and enhance customer lifetime value.

KPI

This KPI analysis evaluates the operational efficiency and service quality of the call center using data from CallCenter.xlsx. Core metrics such as Call Answer Rate, Call Abandonment Rate, Average Talk Time, Average Hold Time, and Longest Wait Time were analyzed alongside advanced KPIs like Average Handling Time (AHT), Calls per Agent, and Agent Utilization Rate. Visualizations reveal consistently high service availability with Call Answer Rates above 99%, occasional spikes in call abandonment, and fluctuating longest wait times during peak days. These insights highlight strong agent performance but also suggest opportunities to optimize staffing and enhance workload management.
